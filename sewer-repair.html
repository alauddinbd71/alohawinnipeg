Know the Warning Signs

Does Your Sewer Line Need Repair?

These symptoms mean your main sewer line may be damaged. Don't ignore them — a small crack becomes a full collapse.

🏠

Sewage Backup in Basement

Water or sewage coming up through floor drains is the most urgent sign. Your main line is blocked or broken.

🌿

Recurring Root Intrusion

Roots come back every year? Your pipe has cracks that invite regrowth. Camera inspection will confirm.

🪣

Multiple Slow Drains

When every fixture drains slowly at the same time, it's your main sewer line — not individual clogs.

💧

Wet Patches in Yard

Soggy grass or sinkholes near your sewer line path indicate a leak underground.

👃

Sewage Smell Indoors

A persistent sewage odour inside your home means cracked pipes are venting sewer gas into your living space.

🐀

Rodent or Pest Problems

Rats and insects enter homes through cracked sewer lines. If pests are increasing, check your line.

What Our Camera Inspection Reveals

  • Exact location and extent of cracks or collapse

Common Questions

Sewer Repair FAQ

How do I know if my sewer line is broken?
Common signs include recurring backups, multiple slow drains, sewage smells, wet patches in the yard, or a sewer line that keeps needing cleaning. A camera inspection confirms the exact issue.
Is the camera inspection really free?
Yes. We perform the inspection at no charge and show you the live footage. If you choose not to proceed with any work, there is no obligation or fee.
Another company said I need to excavate — is that always true?
Not necessarily. Many sewer lines that were quoted for excavation can be relined instead. We've saved customers $2,000–$13,000 over competitors' excavation quotes. Call us for a second opinion — the inspection is free.
How long does sewer line repair take?
A camera inspection takes 30–60 minutes. If pipe relining is the solution, most jobs are completed in a single day. Excavation takes 2–5 days depending on depth and access.
Do you service areas outside Winnipeg?
Yes. We are licensed in Winnipeg, Selkirk, and Brandon, and have traveled 50+ km outside the city for jobs other companies couldn't solve. Call to confirm your area.
What is pipe relining and how long does it last?
CIPP (Cured-in-Place Pipe) relining creates a brand-new seamless pipe inside your existing one — no digging. The liner is rated for 50+ years and is jointless, meaning fewer future failure points.
